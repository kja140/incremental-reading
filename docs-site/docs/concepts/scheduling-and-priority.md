# Scheduling, priority, and A-Factor

Every reading topic can have a due date and a priority. When you grade a topic, the Toolkit schedules its next visit.

**Priority** expresses relative importance. Higher-priority work is favored when several items are available. **A-Factor** controls how quickly the interval grows: it is the reading-topic analogue of an interval growth factor, not a flashcard grade.

The Toolkit can recompute A-Factor from remaining pages or seconds. This makes a long unfinished source return differently from a nearly completed one. Pace adjustments and interval bounds are configurable.

## Practical guidance

- Use priority sparingly; it works best as a relative signal.
- Grade based on the usefulness of the next return, not on guilt about progress.
- Postpone when timing is wrong but the topic still matters.
- Dismiss or mark done when future attention is not worth the queue cost.
- Use **Mercy (spread overdue)** when a backlog has accumulated; it distributes due dates instead of demanding one giant session.
