# What is incremental reading?

Incremental reading is a way to work through many long sources **a little at a time**. Instead of choosing one article or book and forcing yourself to finish it before touching anything else, you keep a queue of useful material and revisit each item in short sessions.

The key idea is simple: **reading, selecting, and remembering are different jobs**.

1. **Read a portion.** Stop when your attention drops, you reach a natural break, or you find something valuable.
2. **Extract the valuable part.** A useful paragraph can become its own scheduled topic.
3. **Turn only durable knowledge into recall material.** A precise question and answer can become a flashcard.
4. **Return later.** The system remembers your place and schedules another encounter.

## Why not just finish the document?

Finishing can be satisfying, but it is not always the best learning goal. A long source may contain only a few ideas worth keeping. Incremental reading lets you:

- follow curiosity without losing your place;
- avoid getting trapped in one difficult source;
- compare ideas from several sources over time;
- give important material more attention than low-value material;
- separate “I saw this” from “I can recall this.”

:::tip A good first goal
Do not try to build a perfect knowledge system. Import one short article, read for five minutes, make one extract, and stop. The return visit is the feature.
:::

## What incremental reading is not

- **Not speed-reading:** comprehension still matters.
- **Not highlighting everything:** extracts should earn future attention.
- **Not making a flashcard for every sentence:** cards are for knowledge you want to retrieve unaided.
- **Not a promise that the queue reaches zero:** a healthy queue can be a garden, not an inbox.

## The feedback loop

```text
source → short reading pass → extract → optional flashcard
   ↑             ↓                         ↓
   └──── scheduled return             spaced review
```

The Toolkit implements the left side of this loop inside Obsidian. The Spaced Repetition community plugin handles flashcard review on the right.
